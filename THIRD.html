Advantages and Limitations

The Nokia 3310, released in 2000, was celebrated for its durability, long battery life, and simplicity. Key advantages included: Its robust build. Long-lasting battery with up to 260 hours of standby time. Innovative SMS features like chat-style messaging and more extended text capabilities. It also allowed for personalization through interchangeable faceplates and came with popular pre-installed games like Snake II. However, the 3310 had limitations, such as basic functionality limited to calls, texts, and simple games. It lacked features that became standard in later phones, including internet access, a camera, and multimedia capabilities. The phone also had a monochrome display and no advanced connectivity options like Bluetooth or Wi-Fi.​
